# TeamFrame — Product Gap Audit

**Date:** 2026-05-30
**Scope:** Every user-facing route under `app/`. Read top-to-bottom, judged against a founder/COO landing cold.
**Method:** No code inventory. Four questions per route:
1. Why would a real customer come here?
2. What can they actually achieve?
3. What's missing or confusing?
4. What does the empty state say to do?

**Outcome:** Ranked list of gaps, then top 3 to ship this week.

## Cross-cutting gaps (not tied to one route)

- **No company-creation flow.** Hardest blocker. Without it, "self-serve" doesn't exist.
- **No notifications layer.** Everything depends on people opening the app at the right moment.
- **No settings page.** Cannot change company name, default timezone, leave policy, branding, working week.
- **No company branding.** Every tenant looks identical.
- **No exports.** Finance ops will ask "give me headcount, leave taken, onboarding completion as a CSV" on day 1.
- **No documents/policies surface.** Schemas exist, UI doesn't.
- **Engineering artefacts on customer surfaces.** Schema visibility panel and limited-telemetry banner on `/employees` must come out before any customer sees the page.

## Ranked gap list

### Bucket 1 — Blocks a demo (would cause "what is this?" mid-call)

1. **Schema visibility + limited-telemetry panels on `/employees`** — visible immediately on the main admin page. Removal is cosmetic, ~1 hour.
2. **No company-creation flow** — if you demo with the seeder, the very first question is "so how do I sign up my company?". You either dodge it or admit it doesn't exist.
3. **Stale callback-failed page (`/auth?error=callback_failed&reason=expired_link`)** — this is the active browser tab right now. The recovery flow is "manually retype your email". Should be one click to resend.

### Bucket 2 — Blocks onboarding a real company (would prevent Arie from using it)

4. **No leave balance / leave type / leave policy** — Arie's finance team cannot run paid leave without an entitlement model. Approving requests in a vacuum is not an HR system.
5. **No onboarding task templates** — re-typing the same checklist for every new hire turns a feature into a chore. Needs per-role packs or at minimum a duplicate-from-last-employee action.
6. **No notifications** — admins won't know a leave request landed without opening the app. Employees won't know they have a new task. Adoption collapses without this.
7. **No employee profile editing on `/me`** — basic self-service expectation. Phone, emergency contact, address.
8. **No CSV export** — finance ops will ask on day 1.

### Bucket 3 — Annoying but survivable

9. **Onboarding task model is title-only** — no due dates, descriptions, attachments. Workable for a 2-week sprint demo, painful long-term.
10. **No team/leave calendar view** — admins manage by spreadsheet today, can keep doing that.
11. **Duplicate org-chart routes** — `/employees` (non-admin) and `/org-chart` show the same thing.
12. **Marketing landing page is too thin** — survivable if all leads come from outbound; blocker if you ever want SEO/inbound.
13. **`/me` becomes empty after week 1** — survivable; employees can live in email.

### Bucket 4 — Nice-to-have

14. **Company branding / logo upload**
15. **Documents & policies surface for employees** (acknowledgements flow)
16. **KPI / funnel dashboard** (your previously planned next feature — useful internally, invisible to customer)
17. **Settings page** (only matters once 14/15 exist)

---

## Recommendation — top 3 to ship this week

If the goal is *"can I demo to Arie's COO next week without flinching?"*, the highest-leverage three are:

1. **Remove the engineering panels from `/employees`** (Gap 1) — half-day, kills the most visible "this looks unfinished" signal.
2. **Add an in-app company-creation flow** (Gap 2) — the bedrock of any self-serve story. Unblocks demos, marketing, every future feature. ~2–3 days.
3. **Add a leave-balance / leave-type model** (Gap 4) — turns the leave queue from a toy into an actual HR feature. Finance firms cannot evaluate without this. ~2–3 days.

Each of these moves a customer outcome. None of them is hardening.

**Alternative top-3 if you'd rather prioritise stickiness over first-impression:**
4 (leave balance) + 5 (onboarding templates) + 6 (notifications) — produces a product that actually does the job day after day, but doesn't fix the "looks unfinished" feeling on first contact.
